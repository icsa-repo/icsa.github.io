Manifesto

Our Manifesto

The Islamic Cyber Security Alliance is a community for people doing offensive and defensive security. The field is understaffed. Attackers move faster than most organizations can keep up with. We exist to put more capable people on the defensive side.

We run workshops, hackathons, and mentorship programs. Someone who has never opened a terminal can sit next to someone who writes exploits for a living and both get something out of it.

A lot of security communities are competitive to a fault. People gatekeep knowledge to protect their own status. We do not do that here. If you know something, you teach it. If you do not know something, you ask.

Prayer breaks are in the schedule. Food is halal. Events do not serve alcohol.

Code of Conduct

Code of Conduct

ICSA is open to anyone, regardless of background, nationality, gender, or skill level.

  • No racism, misogyny, or harassment of any kind, online or in person.
  • All security skills taught here are for defense, research, and authorized testing only.
  • Disagreements are handled with good manners.
  • What is shared in mentorship circles stays confidential.
